🎉 Excited to share that I’ve earned the “DCC August 2024” and “50 Days in 2024” badges on LeetCode!

The DCC badge is for solving the Daily Coding Challenge every day throughout August, while the 50 Days badge recognizes consistency in solving problems over 50 days (even if non-consecutive).

Some of the challenges that stood out to me were:

1. [959: Regions Cut by Slashes](https://leetcode.com/problems/regions-cut-by-slashes/)
2. [664: Stranger Printer](https://leetcode.com/problems/strange-printer/)
3. [564: Find the Closest Palindrome](https://leetcode.com/problems/find-the-closest-palindrome/)
4. [1140: Stone Game II](https://leetcode.com/problems/stone-game-ii/)
5. [1568: Minimum Number of Days to Disconnect Island](https://leetcode.com/problems/minimum-number-of-days-to-disconnect-island/)
6. [1937: Maximum Number of Points with Cost](https://leetcode.com/problems/maximum-number-of-points-with-cost/)
7. [1514: Path with Maximum Probability](https://leetcode.com/problems/path-with-maximum-probability/)
8. [2458: Height of Binary Tree After Subtree Removal Queries](https://leetcode.com/problems/height-of-binary-tree-after-subtree-removal-queries/) – A Google interview problem that had me dreaming of solutions!

These are definitely problems I’ll revisit in the future.

If you’re just starting out on LeetCode, I’d recommend avoiding the DCC challenges as they can be quite tough and at times, discouraging (with this month being about 80% Medium-Hard). Instead, go for the topic-wise study plans. Once you have learned the 80% of the most frequently used data structures and algorithms, you should move to DCC.

It’s a huge time commitment to tackle a *new* problem every day, and there were definitely moments where I felt burnout creeping in. Life has a way of throwing challenges at you, but maintaining daily consistency—whether in coding, fitness, or work—requires persistence. To manage the burnout, I deliberately slowed down, sticking to my mantra: **consistency > intensity**. I’ll admit, I didn’t always follow that mantra perfectly this past month, but it’s a lesson I’m taking forward.

The key is consistency—keep at it, and you’ll see the results. Keep learning, backpropagating, and updating your weights!

With the Fall semester just around the corner, I’m gearing up for the exciting and challenging coursework ahead. It’s going to be hectic and fast-paced—exactly how I like it. If you’re not feeling a bit out of control, you’re not pushing yourself hard enough!
